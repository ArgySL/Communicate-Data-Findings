Investigation Overview¶

I was approached by a third party, let us call the third part "Pokemon trainer Josh", who wanted to gain insightful information about the challenges that he would have to face when facing other Pokemon trainers. Specifically, Josh wants to have only one Pokemon in his party (the maximum allowed is six), so he wanted to know whether he should train a Special Attacker Pokemon, or an Attacker Pokemon.

Dataset Overview¶

This dataset includes 800 Pokemon, in other words, 800 data observations, as well as eleven(11) features, while it utilizes seven(7) of those, one(1) of those being an engineered feature. The aforementioned featured are:

  • attack
  • special_attack
  • speed
  • agility_level
  • total
  • legendary
  • generation

Of the aforementioned features, five(5) are descriptive of Pokemon characteristics(type_1, speed, attack, agility_level, total), while "legendary" and "generation" define certain statuses to the Pokemon. To elaborate on that, "legendary" is a status that defines Pokemon that are unique, as in only one count of each speciment is supposed to exist, whereas "generation" categorizes Pokemon based on their order of Pokemon game chronological release.

Bubbles or Tackles¶

In other words, Special Attack or Attack? This part of the presentation is focused on whether a single-Pokemon trainer (Josh, of course!) should train a pokemon that specializes in dealing damage either via special attacks or attacks.

Bonus: Legendary Pokemon Power Across Generations¶

Every guy or girl who has played Pokemon in their lifetime dreamed of catching and training Legendary pokemon, at least all my friends did! The visualization that follows showcases the power distribution of legendary Pokemon across generations.

Legendary Pokemon made a really strong debut with the first generation, however their powercreep settled down with the second generation, only to become much stronger on the third! The following generations' legendaries were toned down a bit, however they still remained overall strong, compared to their non-legendary counterparts.

To conclude this bonus part, one insight that can be derived from this graph is that the strongest legendary Pokemon belong in the first and third generations, so if you want to catch and train the very best, you should play Pokemon games that include pokemon from these generations!
